Map and Routing Design Principles
‚Ä¢ 	Navigable graph model: represent the maritime domain as a directed graph of nodes and edges where edges are safe navigable corridors (shipping lanes, channels, fairways, buoyed routes) and nodes are waypoints, traffic separation entry/exit points, ports, anchorages, and hazards.
‚Ä¢ 	Continuous-behavior routing: instead of point-to-point straight lines, routes are sequences of corridor segments with associated maneuver instructions (heading changes, speed advisories, depth margins).
‚Ä¢ 	Constraint-aware edges: annotate edges with constraints: depth, width, speed limits, time windows, traffic separation rules, visibility constraints, and ice/weather restrictions.
‚Ä¢ 	Dynamic cost function: compute edge costs from layered inputs: distance, fuel consumption, current/tide assistance, traffic density, port ETA/workload, and safety margin. Costs update in real time.
‚Ä¢ 	Multi-resolution display: at macro zoom show major lanes and traffic density heatmaps; at micro zoom show buoy positions, recommended tracks, and step-by-step turn angles and distances.
